Primer: All or Nothing

Previously, Pikachu built a regression model to do things like weather forecasting. Recently, Nueral Networks (NN) have become quite the hype. Google defines it as "a machine learning model inspired by the human brain, designed to recognize patterns and process data." Quite Novel! Underneath the smoke and mirrors, I am going to tell you a secret. A NN is nothing but a regression model! However, it has a gate. And that gate decides whether to fire an all or nothing response similar to the synapses in our brain.

This chapter will be a precursor to NNs by introducing the most important idea that allows them to work.
Source: studymind

WTF is an Activation Function?

Before talking about activation functions, what are we doing? With NNs, we are basically chaining a bunch of regression models together to hopefully get a meaningful output. We will then install a gating mechanism to say whether we want a model output or not (similar to the brain). To model this all or nothing response, we use something called an activation function. It tells our a nueron (some result from our regression model) whether to let our result through or not. Thus, we have introduced non-linearity to our otherwise linear regression model.
