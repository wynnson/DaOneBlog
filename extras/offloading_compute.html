Offloading Compute


The lazy analogy that I hear a lot regarding AI usage is "the calculator didn't stop people from doing math." After using a calculator, we forget how to do mental math. We become less sharp because we offloaded that computation to a calculator under the belief that it is right (and it rightfully is). Nowadays, when writing emails, we need AI to check it over for us or God forbid, generate it for us. Here is where a calculator and the genie in your pocket differ. If we forget mental math, no big deal, we still learned about the correctness and rarely use it. But if we forget how to write, we forget how to speak for ourselves. You can probably think about many reasons why this is detrimental politically or societally. In Wall-E, the humans on the ship traded their walking ability for the comfort of moving around in a chair. Of course we're are not that dumb (right?). Offloading our brain's compute trades our ability to think for not think. "Not think" is efficient, comfortable, and low-risk. Once you forget, it is time-consuming to remember. How do you expect those who traded their thinking for comfort to even care to remember instead of just using AI again? Between those who know and those who only know with AI, the knowledge disparity will be too great to overcome. There are 2 cases that can play out: 1. AI gets too expensive causing scaling back, or 2. AI getting extremely cheap. In case 1, the outcome is outlined above - the knowledge disparity will be tremendous, becoming class-like. Original thoughts will be of tremendous value. In case 2, the only way to differentiate people is by outsider status. Thus, the world will become more elitist in the years to come no matter the outcome. This megatrend will only get worse. For those seeking hope, know that in all cases, a dominant strategy emerges: to be resiliant to discomfort.
